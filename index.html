About This Project

This project examines Mark Twain’s evolving beliefs about American expansionism and imperialism. Initially supportive of U.S. growth, Twain later became a leading voice against colonialism. Through his letters and writings, we explore how these convictions shaped his literary themes and political activism.

About Mark Twain

Mark Twain (Samuel Langhorne Clemens, 1835–1910) was an American writer, humorist, and social critic. Known for classics like Adventures of Huckleberry Finn and The Adventures of Tom Sawyer, Twain captured the complexities of American life during the 19th century. His later years were marked by outspoken opposition to imperialism, skepticism toward organized religion, and a relentless critique of hypocrisy in society.
